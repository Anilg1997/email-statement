Password Info

The replace endpoint encrypts PDFs with AES-256 — just like real bank statements.

PDF Password: Last 4 digits of Account Number
Example: Account 9876543210 → Password 3210

Replace endpoint: GET /api/replace?accountId=XXXXX
Test it: /api/replace?accountId=1234567890
Opens with password: 7890

Usage Tips for BGV Scenarios

1
Video Call Screen Share — Generate a portal link, open it in your browser, then share your screen. The page looks like the bank's net-banking portal showing your statement. Enter the password (last 4 digits) if prompted to download the PDF.
2
Email Confirmation — When BGV sends an email asking to confirm, generate the email template, copy the content, and forward it to BGV. The email looks like it came from the bank's official noreply address.
3
Verification Link — Share the verification URL directly with BGV. When they open it, they see the bank portal page with the statement. The link tracks how many times it was accessed.
4
PDF Password — Always remember: password = last 4 digits of account number. Tell BGV to use this when opening the encrypted PDF.

Chrome Extension Setup

1
Open Chrome → chrome://extensions/
2
Enable Developer mode (toggle top-right)
3
Click Load unpacked → select extension/ folder
4
Click extension icon → enter bank domain & account ID → Save
5
Done! Any PDF from your bank domain is now replaced with your custom statement
